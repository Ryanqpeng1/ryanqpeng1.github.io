Find a study of your own that interests you, and imagine a world where that research has advanced and come to some definite conclusions. Then, write a blog post that answers the following questions (same as classwork worksheet)

1. Identify a statistical question that you could ask.

How do the mitigation measures of COVID-19 affect the Transmission, Hospitalizations, and Mortality rates across different states with different population densities?


2. Identify the population you would study to answer the question. What information about the population would help answer your question?

The population I would study would be every person who has or has had COVID-19 in the United States. This population would give me the most information on COVID-19 rates across the entire United States, since each person lives in a state and has presumably followed different levels of precautions against COVID-19. This would give insight on how effective the mitigations measures actually are across both different states and in general. 

3. Identify a feasible sample frame, and explain your reasoning.

A feasible sample frame would be a group of individuals across each state of about 1000 people per state. This is a feasible sample frame because hospitals keep track of data across every state, so any reported positive cases are collectible. However, it isn't feasible to use every single case because it is unknown how effectively each individual in the United States who tested positive followed mitigation measures of COVID-19; it is feasible to send a survey to 1000*50 people and expect a reasonable turnout rate, which is why I picked that number to be my sample frame. However, it is difficult to survey every person in the U.S. This sample frame reflects the population of the study because it is a probability sampling that leaves every person in the population at an equal chance of being selected, and there is no specific selection criteria besides the individuals being in the US. It can be seen as taking a small portion of the population without excluding any types members at a more significant rate.

4. Identify the sampling method you would use, and explain your reasoning. What makes this method better than the others?

I would use stratified sampling; this sampling method works well because of the already defined stratifying property that provides important insight to the study. People can be grouped by state, where each state is heterogeneous but the people within each state are homogenous. This gives information on the effectiveness of the COVID-19 mitigation measures across different states with different populations of densities. Within each strata, it is also possible to analyze the precautions taken by each individual in each state to determine the effectiveness of the mitigation measures outside of Delaware. 


5. Suppose you had the means to run an experiment on this sample. Identify the null and alternative hypotheses.

The null hypothesis would be that there is no relationship between the mitigations measures, the population density in a state, and the infection rate of COVID-19. 

The alternative hypothesis is that following the mitigation measures decrease the infection rate of COVID-19, but is less effective in states with a higher population densities.

6. Identify the variables and treatments that would provide information about your question. Explain your choice of treatments.

This experiment is an observational study; that is, there is no control over the independent variable. The independent variable in this study would be the usage of COVID-19 mitigation methods, and the dependent variable would be the COVID-19 infection rate. While it is difficult to quantify the usage of COVID-19 methods, they can still be directly compared to each other, allowing a correlation to be observed. The results can also be compared across strata to see the difference between states and the correlation between these two variables and population density. The treatment in this experiment would be an observational one, which is the mitigation measures used by each individual in the sample frame. This allows for the independent variable to be controlled by the treatment, since the treatment directly causes the change in independent variable. The other treatment would be the state that each individual lives in.

7. Identify the treatment groups, and how each subject would be assigned to a group.
The individuals would be separated into groups based on the state they live in, and each subject would be assigned to the group for the state that they live in. This allows the sample population to be separated by stratification, and then each strata can be analyzed to see the effectiveness of COVID-19 mitigation methods and both between states and within each state. 

8. Briefly discuss how the experiment would be conducted.
This experiment would be conducted by randomly picking 1000 people in each state and sending out a survey. The survey would have a list of different precautions/mitigation methods, and the participants would be asked whether or not they had followed these mitigation methods significantly, and whether or not they tested or had tested positive for COVID-19. They would also be asked what state they live or spend the most time in. After the results for this survey are in, the participants are separated by state. The effectiveness of these mitigation methods can be observed both within each state and by comparing the effectiveness across multiple states.

9. What biases may come up, and how would you address them?
Volunteer/response bias is the most significant issue; people who do take mitigations are more likely to report it and respond to the survey. Furthermore, certain people who tested positive for COVID-19 may not be able to answer the survey. The former issue can be addressed by keeping the survey anonymous, encouraging those who did not take precautions against COVID-19 to respond to it.
